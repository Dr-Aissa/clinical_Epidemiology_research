This methodological guide provides a structured approach for conducting clinical epidemiology research, from question formulation to results publication.

üéØ Methodological Steps

1. Research Question Formulation

Clearly define the objective using the PICO framework (Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome).

Example: In type 2 diabetic patients (P), does basal insulin (I) compared to oral antidiabetics (C) improve glycemic control (O)?

2. Study Design

Select the appropriate study type according to the objective and available resources.

Observational Studies

  • Cohort studies
  • Case-control studies
  • Cross-sectional studies

Experimental Studies

  • Randomized controlled trials
  • Controlled clinical trials
  • Pilot studies

3. Data Collection

Establish rigorous protocols for data collection and management.

Recommended Tools

  • REDCap for electronic collection
  • OpenClinica for clinical trials
  • Structured Excel spreadsheets for small studies
